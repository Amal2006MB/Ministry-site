Kodikunnil Vishnunadh** is an Indian politician and prominent leader of the Indian National Congress from Kerala. Born in Mavadi, Kollam district, to P. Chellappan Pillai and Leela C. Pillai, he completed his education at Devaswom Board College, Sasthamkotta, where he became actively involved in student politics through the Kerala Students Union (KSU). Rising through the ranks of the student movement, he served in several leadership positions, including State President of KSU from 2002 to 2006. He later became President of the Kerala Youth Congress, General Secretary and Vice President of the Kerala Pradesh Congress Committee (KPCC), and Secretary of the Indian National Congress with responsibility for Karnataka affairs. Vishnunadh first entered the Kerala Legislative Assembly in 2006 after winning from the Chengannur constituency and was re-elected in 2011. Although he was defeated in 2016, he made a successful political comeback in 2021 by winning the Kundara constituency. Recognized for his leadership in student and youth movements, organizational skills, and active participation in public issues, Vishnunadh continues to be one of the notable Congress leaders in Kerala politics.
