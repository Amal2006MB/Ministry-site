Kerala is governed by the United Democratic Front (UDF) under the leadership of V. D. Satheesan, who became Chief Minister after the 2026 Kerala Assembly elections. The new ministry consists of 21 members, including the Chief Minister and 20 Cabinet Ministers, representing a mix of experienced leaders and first-time ministers. The government focuses on economic development, infrastructure improvement, job creation, healthcare, education, and social welfare. Key portfolios such as Finance, Law, and General Administration are handled directly by the Chief Minister, while other ministers oversee sectors like Home Affairs, Power, Health, Education, Agriculture, and Public Works. The ministry aims to promote inclusive growth, improve public services, and strengthen Kerala's position as one of India's leading states in human development and social progress.
