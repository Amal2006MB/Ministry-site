V. D. Satheesan (Vadasseri Damodara Menon Satheesan), born on 31 May 1964 in Nettoor, Kochi, is an Indian politician, lawyer, and senior leader of the Indian National Congress who has served as the 13th Chief Minister of Kerala since May 2026. He completed his schooling at Panangad High School and later earned a degree in Sociology from Sacred Heart College, Thevara. He went on to obtain his LL.B. from Kerala Law Academy Law College, an LL.M. from Government Law College, Thiruvananthapuram, and a Post Graduate Diploma in Social Work from Rajagiri College of Social Sciences. Before entering full-time politics, Satheesan practiced law in the Kerala High Court for nearly ten years. His political journey began through the Kerala Students Union (KSU), where he emerged as a student leader and later served in the National Students' Union and Youth Congress. After narrowly losing the 1996 Kerala Assembly election from Paravur, he won the constituency in 2001 and has retained the seat in every subsequent election. In 2021, he became the Leader of the Opposition in the Kerala Legislative Assembly and gained recognition for his strong criticism of the ruling government and his efforts to modernize the Congress party in Kerala. Under his leadership, the United Democratic Front (UDF) achieved a historic victory in the 2026 Kerala Legislative Assembly election, winning 102 of the 140 seats and ending a decade of Left Democratic Front rule. Following this success, he was elected leader of the Congress Legislature Party and sworn in as the Chief Minister of Kerala on 18 May 2026. As Chief Minister, he oversees several key departments, including Finance, Law, General Administration, Disaster Management, and Ports. Known for his confident, reform-oriented, and people-centric leadership style, Satheesan is regarded as one of the most influential political leaders in contemporary Kerala politics.
