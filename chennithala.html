Ramesh Chennithala (born 25 May 1956) is an Indian politician, lawyer, and prominent leader of the Indian National Congress from Kerala who currently serves as the Minister for Home, Vigilance, and Coir Industries in the Kerala government. Born in Chennithala village in Alappuzha district to V. Ramakrishnan Nair and Devakiamma, he completed his schooling at Mahatma High School, Chennithala, earned a Bachelor's degree in Economics from NSS Hindu College, Changanassery, and later obtained an LL.B. degree from Government Law College, Thiruvananthapuram. He entered politics at a very young age through the Kerala Students Union (KSU), serving in several leadership positions before becoming the State President of KSU in 1980. His leadership abilities soon earned him national recognition, leading to his appointment as the All India President of the National Students Union of India (NSUI) in 1982. In the same year, he was elected to the Kerala Legislative Assembly from Haripad constituency, marking the beginning of a long and distinguished political career. He later became General Secretary of the Indian Youth Congress and, at the age of 28, became one of the youngest ministers in Kerala when he was appointed Minister for Rural Development in the cabinet of K. Karunakaran in 1986. Chennithala subsequently expanded his political influence at the national level, serving multiple terms as a Member of Parliament representing the Kottayam and Mavelikkara constituencies. During his tenure in Parliament, he gained recognition for his effective speeches and active participation in legislative discussions and parliamentary committees. He held several important organizational positions within the Congress party, including AICC Secretary, National President of the Indian Youth Congress, and President of the Kerala Pradesh Congress Committee (KPCC). He also became a member of the prestigious Congress Working Committee, the highest decision-making body of the Indian National Congress. In 2005, he returned to state politics as KPCC President and played a crucial role in strengthening the Congress organization in Kerala. In 2011, Chennithala was re-elected as MLA from Haripad and, in 2014, assumed office as Kerala's Home Minister in the Oommen Chandy government. During his tenure, he introduced several notable initiatives, including Operation Kubera to curb illegal money lending and the Clean Campus Safe Campus project to combat drug abuse among students. He later served as the Leader of the Opposition in the 14th Kerala Legislative Assembly, where he became one of the strongest voices against the ruling government and actively raised issues related to governance, corruption, and public welfare. He also took a firm stand on national issues such as the Citizenship Amendment Act (CAA) and the proposed National Register of Citizens (NRC), advocating for constitutional values and social harmony. Following the historic victory of the Congress-led United Democratic Front (UDF) in the 2026 Kerala Legislative Assembly election, Chennithala retained his Haripad seat and was inducted into the cabinet headed by Chief Minister V. D. Satheesan. He was entrusted with the key portfolios of Home, Vigilance, and Coir Industries. As Home Minister, he launched Operation Toofan, a large-scale initiative aimed at eliminating drug trafficking and organized crime across Kerala through coordinated action by the police and public participation. Widely respected for his administrative experience, organizational skills, and decades of public service, Ramesh Chennithala remains one of the most influential and experienced political leaders in Kerala, with a career spanning over four decades in both state and national politics.
